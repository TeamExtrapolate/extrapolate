The dataset contained a field of College Tier where aptitude score annotated each college with a score of 1 and 2. The annotations have been computed from the average scores obtained by the students in the college/university as well as the institute's reputation. Colleges with an average score above a threshold are tagged as 1 and others as 2. College City Tier is annotated based on the population of the city in which the college is located.

In our observation, Tier 1 college candidates have secured more salary on an average than Tier 2 college, which coincides with the general trend, whereas the college city tier does not affect salary greatly.

Most of the salary is accumulated around 3 to 5 LPA region, which is the average salary obtained by Indian (engineering) candidates. Few outliers are present above 10 LPA.

Contrary to the popular notion, there is little correlation between College GPA & the salary offered.

For a specified designation, Software Engineer in this case, the set of values of salaires lying between (Q3 + 1.5IQR) and (Q1 - 1.5IQR) were considered to exclude the outliers. The mean of these values was computed and treated as a benchmark. Individuals receiving salaries less than this mean were said to be underemployed.

As a result, it was observed that nearly 51% of all Software Engineers are UNDEREMPLOYED.
